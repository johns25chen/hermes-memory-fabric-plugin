## 8. Source-to-Human-Decision Separation Model

The source-to-human-decision model is a set of separations, not a chosen flow. A source may remain a source. Evidence may remain insufficient. A candidate may be rejected or returned without becoming a proposal. A proposal may never enter review. Review may produce HOLD or STOP and cannot approve. An approval request may be rejected, returned, held, or deferred. Approval remains scoped and cannot authorize. Authorization, if separately created in later work, cannot adopt, mutate, or execute. Handoff carries context only.

At every conceptual boundary:

- provenance remains attached to the bounded claim or context;
- uncertainty remains visible and is not converted into confidence or truth;
- material conflict remains visible and produces HOLD;
- an earlier state is not equivalent to a later state;
- PASS confirms only boundary preservation and performs no transition;
- Human Owner authority cannot be inferred from a role, tool, carrier, document, review, status, merge, or silence;
- rejection, return, deferral, HOLD, and STOP remain possible later human responses;
- no sequence is canonical, approved, validated, recommended, final, executable, or production-ready.
