## 10. Workstream Sequence and Human Gating

The only permitted abstract documentary flow is `separate Human Owner instruction -> bounded source review -> evidence extraction -> labeled design hypotheses -> boundary review -> human review -> documentary decision record`. No arrow is automatic.

At every documentary gate, `PASS` means only that the declared documentary criteria are met and the artifact may be presented for the next separately instructed human decision; `HOLD` means work pauses with evidence preserved until a material gap or ambiguity is resolved by the Human Owner; `STOP` means scope or authority has been breached and no successor action may be inferred. A gate result never creates approval, authorization, execution, or another task.

Separate Human Owner instruction is required before every child task. Completion, merge, `PASS`, or closure of one artifact cannot start another.

## 12. Evidence and Decision Standards

Every factual claim must be traceable to the fixed corpus. Any proposition not established by that corpus must be clearly labeled `DESIGN-HYPOTHESIS`, including assumptions about users, demand, usability, adoption, deployment, willingness to pay, pricing, launch, technical feasibility, operational outcomes, or external-candidate usefulness.

Evidence is not truth; a design hypothesis is not evidence; review is not approval; an approval request is not approval; approval is not authorization; authorization is not adoption, mutation, or execution. Confidence must be presented with provenance, limits, contrary evidence, and unresolved gaps. Documentary quality is judged by traceability, identity separation, internal consistency, explicit uncertainty, non-overreach, and human-review readiness, not automated scoring or ranking.

A gate is `PASS` only with complete traceability and no material contradiction. It is `HOLD` when a material documentary gap exists. It is `STOP` when a claim is invented, authority is inferred, or the fixed corpus is exceeded without new Human Owner scope.
