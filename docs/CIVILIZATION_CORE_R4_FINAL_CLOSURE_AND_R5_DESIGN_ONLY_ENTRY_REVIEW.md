# Civilization Core R4 Final Closure and R5 Design-Only Entry Review

## 1. Review Status

This document is docs-only, design-only, closure-review-only, and entry-review-only. It consolidates R4.1 through R4.5 without changing their source documents or creating runtime behavior.

The final decisions are:

| Decision | Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| R4 corridor closure | `CLOSE` | The five bounded R4 design and closure records are coherent and formally closed. |
| R5 design-only entry | `GO` | A separately human-scoped R5 roadmap design workstream may begin under inherited boundaries. GO is not implementation permission. |
| R5 runtime implementation | `DEFER` | No R5 runtime implementation is authorized. |

## 2. Purpose

The purpose is to determine whether the R4 corridor has completed its documentary closure obligations and whether R5 design-only work may enter a bounded roadmap corridor. This review records closure and entry decisions; it does not approve, authorize, execute, deploy, route, persist, mutate, score, rank, validate, or enforce runtime behavior.

## 11. Cross-Workstream Coherence

The five records share one governance chain: source material supports evidence; evidence supports a candidate; a candidate may enter human review; review does not itself create approval; approval does not imply authorization; authorization does not imply execution. Each workstream preserves provenance, uncertainty, explicit human scope, non-authority of tools, and reversible documentary disposition.

No R4 workstream relies on another to supply hidden runtime capability. Compilation output may inform recall design, recall evidence may inform evaluation design, operations vocabulary may describe bounded handling, and evaluation vocabulary may clarify interpretation, but none of those relationships activates execution.

## 12. Vocabulary and Authority Inheritance

The inherited separation remains:

`source → evidence → candidate → proposal → review → approval request → approval → authorization → adoption → execution`

No arrow is automatic. A document, test, audit, benchmark, score, model output, tool output, closure decision, or entry decision cannot grant authority. Human sovereignty and explicit human scope remain mandatory.

## 13. Evidence and Provenance Inheritance

Every future design candidate must identify its source, extraction or derivation context, scope, uncertainty, gaps, and human review role. Evidence is support, not proof of authority. Provenance is traceability, not correctness. Missing provenance must remain visible rather than being inferred or fabricated.

R4 closure preserves raw sources and historical wording. It neither rewrites the corpus nor converts documentary objects into stored records.

## 14. Candidate Object and Lifecycle Alignment

Candidate objects remain conceptual and non-authoritative. Their lifecycle is bounded to explicit scope, source review, evidence extraction, candidate design, boundary review, human review, and decision record. Deferral stays deferred; rejection stays recorded; expiration requires new explicit human scope.

An object name is not a schema, an enum, a database record, a graph node, an API contract, or a runtime type. A lifecycle label is not a workflow engine or state machine.
