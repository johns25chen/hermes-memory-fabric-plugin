## 6. Documentary Contract Definition and Method

A conceptual documentary contract is a prose-only, independently reviewable boundary that states documentary purpose, bounded responsibility, source or evidence position, prohibited interpretation, human decision boundary, and stop condition.

The word contract does not imply legal enforceability, machine enforcement, runtime behavior, or implementation commitment.

- DCM-01 — A conceptual documentary contract describes a bounded responsibility in prose.
- DCM-02 — Each contract states what documentary concern it covers and what it excludes.
- DCM-03 — Each contract identifies its source or evidence relationship without creating stored evidence.
- DCM-04 — Each contract identifies its authority or non-authority position without granting authority.
- DCM-05 — Each contract states prohibited interpretations that would create scope or authority drift.
- DCM-06 — Each contract names the required Human Owner decision boundary.
- DCM-07 — Each contract states a stop condition for boundary or evidence failure.
- DCM-08 — Each contract ends with a non-executing documentary handoff.
- DCM-09 — Contract language must not be converted into typed fields, commands, events, methods, or transitions.
- DCM-10 — Contract completeness remains a human review question and never becomes automatic validation or enforcement.
