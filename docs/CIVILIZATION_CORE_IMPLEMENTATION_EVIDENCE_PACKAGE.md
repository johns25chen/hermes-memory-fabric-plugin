## 3. Evidence Vocabulary and Non-Substitution Rules

The required vocabulary is:

- `SUBSTANTIVE-EVIDENCE`: existing evidence that directly supports a product, security, technical, operational, commercial, deployment, or release proposition. No row in this package establishes such evidence as sufficient.
- `BOUNDARY-EVIDENCE`: documentary evidence of governance, authority, non-action, separation, or scope limits.
- `PROVENANCE-DESIGN`: a documentary design for source and provenance visibility; it is not provenance validation or substantive evidence.
- `CLOSED-STATE-EVIDENCE`: documentary evidence preserving a closed, sealed, deferred, undecided, or absent-authority state.
- `DOCUMENTARY-EVIDENCE-OF-GAP`: a traceable record that evidence is absent, insufficient, unknown, held, deferred, or undecided.
- `MISSING`: the named substantive evidence is absent from the fixed corpus.
- `INSUFFICIENT`: the fixed corpus does not establish enough substantive evidence for the named proposition.
- `UNDECIDED`: no threshold or decision has been selected.
- `DEFER`: the decision remains deferred without promotion.

A boundary, checklist, design, closure, or gap record is not relabeled as substantive product, security, technical, operational, commercial, deployment, or release evidence. Documentary evidence that a gap exists is not the missing substantive evidence. Listing evidence does not establish its sufficiency. The source expressly states that documentary history and boundary records do not establish implementation, product, security, privacy, technical, operational, deployment, commercial, launch, release, or change-control readiness. Source: `docs/CIVILIZATION_CORE_HISTORICAL_MACRO_R6_IMPLEMENTATION_AND_RELEASE_ELIGIBILITY_DECISION_FRAMES.md`, section `4. Governing Source Corpus`.
